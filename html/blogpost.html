Navigating CCIT & TCS: A Retrospective Guide

General Advise

  • Aim to acquire as many General Credits as possible in your first year to graduate early. This is feasible due to the overlapping courses between CCIT and TCS courses, which allows for quicker fulfillment of graduation requirements.
  • Try to fulfill your Distribution Requirements in your first year. This aligns with the strategy of accumulating as many General Credits as possible for faster graduation.
  • When applying to the CCIT or TCS programs, ensure you have 4.0 UTM credits. High school transfer credits do not meet program requirements.
  • To enroll in TCS, you must pass CCT109, CCT110, CCT111 each with a minimum final grade of 65%.

Disclaimer

This guide is not the ultimate resource for your progression through CCIT and TCS combined majors. The information included is based on my personal experiences as a current third year student (2023), so an individual's experience of courses may vary.

Background

To provide context for my course evaluations, my interests and background largely lie in information technology and multimedia. My education and professional experiences have focused on IT, web development, and graphic design. I have also dabbled in live stream production and film studies. These interests influence the CCIT & TCS courses I've taken and plan to take.

Another factor in my course selection is my preference for certain types of assessments. I tend to avoid courses with midterms or final exams. Instead, I prefer those that feature final projects, programming tasks, weekly labs, and individual work.

If your motivations align with mine, you may find my experiences and insights applicable to your situation, offering practical strategies for your university life.

Communication, Culture, Information & Technology (CCIT)

Second Year

First Requirement

CCT204 - Design Thinking I ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This is straightforward, with a focus on group work and projects. It includes individual assignments utilizing various Adobe software. I am glad that there was no final examination.

CCT208 - Communications Research Methods ⭐⭐⭐

Attendance at tutorials is critical as they contribute significantly to the final grade. The course involves essay work which can be easily done with the posted slides. Also, it luckily does not have a final examination. This course overlaps with TCS.

CCT218 - Foundations of Media and Technology Studies ❓

This course follows CCT109/110, considered less intense in Summer. It involves essays and a final exam. I aim to take it in Summer due to the 40% weightage of the final exam during Fall and Winter.

Second Requirement

Choose a course of interest. I left the less engaging ones for last. Note that these courses don't overlap with TCS, potentially reducing their appeal. I'm likely to take CCT206 next, with CCT210 as a second option.

  1. CCT200 - Race, Media and Culture
  1. CCT206 - Law, Technology and Culture 👈
  1. CCT210 - Signs, Referents, and Meaning
  1. CCT222 - Political Economy of Communication, Culture, and Technology

Third Requirement

CCT212 - Hacker Culture ⭐⭐

This course focuses on learning about hackers, hacker groups, and online activism. It is lecture-intensive and concludes with a group essay and final examination. Personally, it was a bit boring and attendance was not required.

CCT260 - Web Development and Design I

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This is an enjoyable and straightforward course where you learn the fundamentals of HTML/CSS for website creation. It includes take-home labs, two web based projects, and a multiple-choice midterm and final examination.

Note: I recommend taking either CCT212 or CCT205 because they help satisfy CCIT and TCS requirements. Also, CCT260 was chosen because of my interest in website development and UI/UX. Follow-on courses CCT360 and CCT460 fulfill Higher Year requirements.

Fourth Requirement

CCT250 - Technology and Creative Expression ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Changes have been made since I last took this course. It is currently reported to be less challenging. It involves learning Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator and completing individual projects.

Note: For this requirement, you're not obligated to take CCT250. You can select any 200-level course, provided it's not part of the required courses for CCIT major.

Higher Years (3rd & 4th year)

CCT353 - Digital Media: Video

⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course introduces film production, including work with equipment and Adobe Premiere Pro for editing and creating short films. No final exam is included.

CCT360 - Web Development and Design II

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course builds on HTML/CSS, introducing Bootstrap and JavaScript. It features in-class labs, homework, participation grades, a midterm, and two web-based projects, with no final exam.

Technology, Coding & Society (TCS)

Second Year

First Requirement

CCT208 - Communications Research Methods ⭐⭐⭐

Attendance at tutorials is critical as they contribute significantly to the final grade. The course involves essay work and does not have a final examination. This course overlaps with CCIT.

CCT211 - Fundamentals of User Interface Programming ⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course includes using Python to create 2D pixel games, progressing to developing software applications with an introduction to databases (SQL). It also involves programming labs, individual or group exercises, final projects, and in-class programming midterm and final exams.

CCT212 - Hacker Culture ⭐⭐

This course focuses on learning about hackers, hacker groups, and online activism. It is lecture-intensive and concludes with a group essay and final examination. This course overlaps with CCIT.

CCT285 - Immersive Environment Design

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This enjoyable, slightly challenging course involves game building using Unity. Some programming experience may be beneficial. The course is project-based and does not include a final examination.

CCT205 - Digital Innovation and Cultural Transformation ❓

I haven't yet taken 'CCT205 - Digital Innovation and Cultural Transformation' because I prioritized other courses that I found more intriguing. According to the previous syllabus, the course includes a midterm and a final exam, multiple group essays, and a small participation grade.

Second Requirement

CCT286 - Interactive Media Design ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course involves group work and real-world projects, primarily focused on web development and deployment. It concludes with a final reflection instead of an exam.

Note: I selected CCT286 as it seemed more interesting and I already had the CCT285 prerequisite. Unlike CCT221 and CCT226, CCT286 does not require CCT112, which is not a prerequisite for TCS. CCT202 could serve as an alternative to CCT286 as CCT202 has easier prerequisites.

Third and Higher Years

First Requirement

CCT320 - Communication, Technology, and Social Change ⭐⭐⭐

This course covers AI, migration, and activism, with a podcast group project instead of a final exam. Attendance isn't graded, but individual essays are required. Prerequisites include either CCT212, CCT218, or CCT222.

CCT380 - Human-Computer Interaction and Communication ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course, similar to CCT286, involves a group project to redesign a poorly designed website. It includes in-class workshops and lecture content explains how to plan and develop a website by engaging in user research.

CCT432 - Ethics and Code ❓

I haven't taken this course yet, so I can't provide an opinion. However, according to the most recent syllabus (Fall 2023), it includes multiple essays, group presentations, quizzes, and mandatory attendance.

CCT477 - UX Design: Understanding Users

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

This course, potentially subject to future changes, offers an engaging study of qualitative user research, culminating in a final project. It includes presentations, an essay, weekly reflections, and does not require a final exam.
